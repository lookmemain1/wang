1. Purpose and how engineers should use this transcript
This document is a synthetic transcript of a single outpatient consultation between an Indonesian general practitioner and a patient under the BPJS Prolanis (chronic disease management) programme. Total duration is approximately 5 minutes 10 seconds. The transcript is calibrated against the Template 1 (Outpatient Consultation Note) in CognitasLabs_Demo-Templates_v1.docx — every clinical fact stated in the conversation maps to a structured field in that template.
Engineers should use this asset for three purposes:
•	Ambient-capture test input — feed the raw text (or a voice-acted recording of it) into the speech-to-text + diarisation stack and measure transcription accuracy on mixed-language clinical Indonesian.
•	Extraction test input — feed the clean transcript into the clinical-NER and SOAP-structuring layer; the Section 4 ground-truth output is the expected structured note.
•	End-to-end demo script — use the conversation verbatim for the demo presentation, with the AI generating the structured note live in front of the audience.
Language and style notes
•	Primary language: Bahasa Indonesia. Medical terms, drug names, lab values, and brief functional words (oke, anyway, basically, compliance, target) are code-switched in English/Latin. This reflects how Indonesian doctors actually speak.
•	The doctor uses honorifics (Pak, Bapak) and warm informal register throughout — this is realistic and the model should not 'over-formalise' the output.
•	Disfluencies are preserved: false starts (eh, anu), filler words (hmm, oke), short interruptions, and brief overlaps. These should be normalised in the structured output, not in the raw transcript.
•	Speaker labels: DOKTER (general practitioner, female ~35 y/o voice), PASIEN (male, 58 y/o, soft-spoken). One brief utterance attributed to SUSTER (nurse) for vitals callout.
•	Timestamps: minutes:seconds from start, advanced realistically based on speech rate of approximately 140-160 words per minute in conversational Bahasa with pauses for examination.

5.2  Demo feature checkpoints
Feature 1 — Ambient note-taking
•	Speaker diarisation: 3 distinct speakers (DOKTER, PASIEN, SUSTER), one nurse turn embedded in physician dialogue.
•	Code-switching robustness: 21 English/Latin medical terms within Bahasa sentences (compliance, target, vertigo, orthostatic hypotension, diabetic neuropathy, eGFR, microalbuminuria, UACR, etc.).
•	Number normalisation: '7 koma 4' must become 7.4; '148 per 92' must become 148/92; '500 milligram tiga kali sehari' must become 500 mg TID.
•	Disfluency handling: 'anu', 'eh', filler 'hmm', false starts, soft laughter — all to be removed from structured output, preserved in raw transcript.
Feature 2 — Insurance coding
•	Primary diagnosis selection: E11.9 (T2DM without complications) is the correct primary today because DKD is not yet confirmed (urinalysis only, no UACR).
•	Candidate upgrade flag: if next-visit UACR confirms ≥30 mg/g, AI should auto-suggest upgrading to E11.21 (T2DM with diabetic nephropathy). This shifts the INA-CBG group.
•	Secondary diagnoses must include I10, E78.2, E66.3 — all three are in the patient's record AND addressed in today's plan, so they belong on the claim.
•	I95.1 (orthostatic hypotension) is mentioned conversationally but only as 'probable' — AI should flag this as a working/observation diagnosis, not coded for claim until objectively measured (active stand BP test).
Feature 3 — Drug-interaction and prescribing alerts
•	Captopril + Aspirin: low-grade advisory (reduced ACE-I efficacy, renal vasoconstriction risk in dehydration) — should surface as ADVISORY, not blocking.
•	Simvastatin + Amlodipine: limit Simvastatin to ≤20 mg per FDA 2011 advisory. Current 20 mg is at the ceiling — flag as MONITORED, not blocking.
•	Glimepiride in 58 y/o with eGFR 78: acceptable now, but flag for re-evaluation if eGFR falls below 60.
•	New-patient orthostatic symptoms on 5 antihypertensive-modulating drugs (Amlodipine, Captopril, Glimepiride, Simvastatin, Aspirin): HIGH advisory for polypharmacy contribution. Suggest staged deprescribing if symptoms persist.
•	Future Captopril → Lisinopril switch (stated by doctor): AI should record this as a 'planned medication change' linked to the conditional 'if BP uncontrolled next visit' — useful for clinical decision support continuity.
